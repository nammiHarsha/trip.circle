Vietnam City Hop

 

Duration 8 Days
Group Size 15–20 people
Stay 3/4 star hotels
Trip Type Group Trip

About This Trip

Vietnam is one of those destinations that has become a must-visit in your 20s(or 30s)! It has the pace of Thailand while also offering unique experiences. Also, a paradise for coffee lovers - matcha lovers also would love the coffee here. Legend says that Indian cafe owners are taking long trips here to understand how the Vietnamese Cold Coffee is brewed!

Brief Itinerary

Day 1 – Arrival in Ho Chi Minh City
Land in the pulsating heart of southern Vietnam and transfer to your centrally located hotel. Freshen up and head out to capture the colonial grandeur of the Notre-Dame Cathedral and the historic Central Post Office. Explore the backpacker buzz of Bui Vien Walking Street — a neon corridor of open-air bars, live music, and local vendors.
Day 2 – Cu Chi Tunnels & City History
We visit the Cu Chi Tunnels — crawl through narrow passages, examine hidden trapdoors, and fire authentic period weapons at the shooting range. Ascend to a rooftop bar for sweeping panoramas of the Ho Chi Minh skyline.
Day 3 – Ho Chi Minh City to Hoi An
Fly north to Da Nang and transfer to the timeless Hoi An Ancient Town. We will stop by the Marble Mountains before checking into our hotel. In the evening, float a flower lantern down the Thu Bon River, browse the vibrant night market, and feast on Hoi An's signature dishes.
Day 4 – Ba Na Hills & the Golden Bridge
We head out on a day trip to Ba Na Hills where the crown jewel awaits: the Golden Bridge! Explore the French Village, and the Fantasy Park - the roller coaster and dashing cars are a must do!
Day 5 – Basket Boat Experience
We head out to the coconut palm-lined waterways of the Cam Thanh Village for a basket boat experience — spin in traditional round coracles through narrow canals. Cycle through Hoi An at golden hour, and maybe visit a local tailor to get a custom suit or dress made (a popular thing to do here).
Day 6 – Hoi An to Hanoi & Train Street
Fly north to Hanoi, check-in to the hotel right in the middle of the bustling Old Quarter. Freshen up and explore the 36 Streets Old Quarter. Later, we head to the hidden entrance of the infamous train street, where a train runs through a busy narrow market street!
Day 7 – Halong Bay Kayaking & Return to Hanoi
Board your junk cruise into Halong Bay — a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Settle into your cabin, enjoy a welcome lunch on deck, and kayak into hidden lagoons and sea caves accessible only by paddle. Head back to Hanoi and end the evening with some fresh beer.
Day 8 – Departure from Hanoi
After a final Vietnamese breakfast, transfer to Noi Bai International Airport carrying the full weight of Vietnam's beauty, history, and spirit.
